# Battleship: Predictive Analysis Variant

## Objective
Sink all the opponent’s ships while managing dynamic ship placement. Players must eliminate the impossible while avoiding the impossible themselves.

---

## Setup

- Grid Size: 10×10 grid
    - One grid for your ships
    - One grid for tracking the opponent’s shots

- Ships (standard set):
    - 1 Aircraft Carrier (5 spaces)
    - 1 Battleship (4 spaces)
    - 1 Submarine (3 spaces)
    - 1 Cruiser (3 spaces)
    - 1 Destroyer (2 spaces)

- Ships are not placed at the start of the game

---

## Gameplay

### 1. Ship Placement
- No initial ship placement
- Ships are placed during the game only when needed
- Ships must:
    - Be placed horizontally or vertically
    - Not overlap
    - Not extend off the grid

---

### 2. Calling Shots
- Players take turns calling a grid position (e.g., "E5")
- The opponent declares whether it is a hit or a miss

---

### 3. Ship Sinks
- Declare a ship as sunk once all its positions are hit
- No need to identify which ship was sunk—just declare that a ship is sunk

---

### 4. Legal Ship Placement
- Players must ensure ships are legally placed at all times
    - No overlaps
    - No off-grid placement
- If a player cannot place all ships legally, they lose the game

---

## Winning

- The first player to sink all of their opponent’s ships wins
- If a player cannot legally place all ships at any point, they lose

---

In "Battleship: Predictive Analysis Variant," players manage dynamic ship positions, adapt strategies, and predict their opponent’s next move. This variant has flexible placement and delayed sinking.
